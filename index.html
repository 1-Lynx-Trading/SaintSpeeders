The Competition

What is H2GP?

The world's premier hydrogen fuel cell racing competition for students — where engineering meets endurance.

Hydrogen Fuel Cell Technology

Students design, build, and race remote-controlled cars powered by hydrogen fuel cells — converting chemical energy directly into electricity with zero emissions.

Global Competition

Teams from around the world compete in endurance races, testing not just speed but efficiency, reliability, and strategic energy management.

Why Hydrogen Matters

Hydrogen is the most abundant element in the universe. As a fuel, it produces only water — representing the future of clean energy and sustainable transportation.

Development Pipeline

Engineering & Development

From concept to competition — our systematic approach to building a championship contender.

Our design process begins with extensive research into aerodynamic principles, weight distribution, and hydrogen fuel cell integration. Using industry-standard CAD software and computational fluid dynamics, we iterate through dozens of design concepts before selecting the optimal configuration.

CAD Design Completion100%
CFD Analysis95%
Design Review90%
CAD Render — H2-01 Body

Every component undergoes rigorous testing before integration. We test fuel cell performance under varying loads, validate structural integrity through finite element analysis, and conduct wind tunnel sessions to verify aerodynamic predictions.

Fuel Cell Testing85%
Structural Analysis80%
Wind Tunnel Session #12

Manufacturing combines advanced 3D printing for complex geometries, carbon fiber composite layup for the chassis and body, and precision CNC machining for critical components. Each part is quality-checked before assembly begins.

Post-test data is analyzed to identify performance bottlenecks. We track power output, hydrogen consumption, motor temperatures, and lap times to continuously optimize every aspect of the car's performance.

Race strategy is developed through data-driven analysis. We plan hydrogen refueling windows, energy management profiles for different race phases, and communication protocols to ensure flawless race-day execution.
